    shamenoun

    uk ​ /ʃeɪm/ us ​ /ʃeɪm/

    shame noun (BAD LUCK)

    A2 [ S ] If something is described as a shame, it is disappointing or not satisfactory.

    可惜，遺憾
    [ + that ] It's a (great/real) shame that the event had to be cancelled. 音樂會被迫取消，真是（很）遺憾。
    [ + to infinitive ] Have some more vegetables - it would be a shame to waste them. 多吃點蔬菜吧——浪費就太可惜了。
    "Douglas had to miss the school concert because he was ill." "Oh, what a shame/that's a shame!" 「道格拉斯因病不能參加學校音樂會了。」「哦，那真是太遺憾了！」

    More examples

    • What a shame that you couldn't go to the party.
    • "I'm afraid I can't come tonight." " Oh, that's a shame."
    • It's such a shame they shut that factory down.
    • "Go on, finish off this tart, Paul." "Well, it seems a shame to let it go to waste."
    • It seems a shame to miss such a nice occasion.

    shame noun (BAD FEELING)

    C1 [ U ] an uncomfortable feeling of guilt or of being ashamed because of your own or someone else's bad behaviour

    羞恥，羞愧
    He said he felt no shame for what he had done. 他說他並不爲自己的所作所爲感到羞愧。
    The children hung/bowed their heads in shame. 孩子們羞愧地低下了頭。
    The shame of the scandal was so great that he shot himself a few weeks later. 這件醜聞讓他無地自容，所以幾個星期後他開槍自殺了。
    You can't go out dressed like that - have you no shame (= don't you feel ashamed about being dressed like that)? 你不能穿成這樣就出去——你難道不怕丟人嗎？

    › [ U ] loss of honour and respect

    丟臉；恥辱
    He thinks there's great shame in being out of work and unable to provide for his family. 他認爲沒有工作、無法養活家人是一件很丟臉的事情。
    In some societies, if a woman leaves her husband, it brings shame on her and her family. 在有些社會裡，如果婦女離開自己的丈夫，這會給她和她的家人帶來恥辱。
    put sb to shame

    › UK to make someone feel ashamed

    使（某人）感到羞愧
    It puts me to shame that I still haven't replied to David's letter. 我還沒有給戴維回信，真讓我慚愧。
    to my shame

    › I feel ashamed because

    令（我）羞愧的是
    To my shame, I never wrote and thanked Mary for her present. 令人羞愧的是，我從沒有給瑪麗寫信，感謝她送的禮物。
    shame on you

    › used to tell someone that they should feel sorry for something they did

    （你）應該感到羞愧
    Shame on you for being so mean. 你如此刻薄，真該感到羞愧。
    humorous You were in town and you didn't come and see us - shame on you! 你是說你來過城裡卻沒有來看我們——真不像話！

    More examples

    • He knew he'd done something wrong and hung his head in shame.
    • He went scarlet with shame and embarrassment.
    • Her face burned with shame.
    • The shame of losing a game of tennis to my mother!
    • I couldn't treat anyone so badly - I couldn't bear the shame.

    shame noun (COMPARE WELL)

    put sb/sth to shame

    › to make someone or something seem not good by comparison

    使相形見絀
    Your cooking puts mine to shame. 你做菜的功夫令我自慚形愧。

    Idiom(s)

    die of shame

    shameverb [ T ]

    uk ​ /ʃeɪm/ us ​ /ʃeɪm/

    shame verb [ T ] (BAD FEELING)

    › to make someone feel ashamed, or to make someone or something lose honour and respect

    使感到羞愧；使丟臉；羞辱
    It shames me that I treated her so badly. 我對她這麽壞，真是令我羞愧。
    The behaviour of a few children has shamed the whole school. 少數學生的行爲使整個學校都蒙受恥辱。
    shame sb into/out of sth

    › to cause someone to do or not to do something by making them feel ashamed

    使（某人）感到羞愧而做／不做…
    [ + -ing verb ] The city council was shamed into taking action after criticism in the national and local media.

    shame verb [ T ] (COMPARE WELL)

    › to be so much better than something else that the other thing seems of a low standard by comparison

    使相形見絀
    The school's exam results shame those of the other schools in the area. 該校的考試成績使這個地區的其他學校都相形見絀。

    shameexclamation

    uk ​ /ʃeɪm/ us ​ /ʃeɪm/ mainly UK

    › used to express disapproval of something that a public speaker is saying

    （用於表示不贊同演講者所說的話）可恥
    To cries of "Shame!", the minister announced that taxes were being increased. 在一片「可恥」的指責聲中，部長宣佈加稅。
